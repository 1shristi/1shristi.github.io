All-in-one shipping and marketplace solution

to replace a broken legacy system

overview small team | lead designer | 4 weeks

Due to a lack of consistent updates over time, my client's legacy system for shipment had become bloated and a bottleneck causing inefficiencies that negatively impacting their bottom line.

With a comprehensive product roadmap for the new shipping interface ready to go, I was brought in as a contractor to lead user research and design by defining, adapting, and applying user flows from the legacy system into an efficient user-friendly interface that could handle the complexities of international shipping within the client's permissioned B2B marketplace business model.

proposal

I presentated a timeline of four dedicated weeks following the standard design process which cycles through research, ideation, prototyping, and testing. The end result would be a series of UI screens ready for presentation to the stakeholders and c-executives of the legacy system.

Why just 4 weeks?

The client wanted to leverage a visual solution to positively influence and steer the discussion towards budgeting and resourcing for the entire product roadmap in the fourth quarter.

From past experience, I knew the scope of the project could easily extend the original timeline so I made sure the team understood that design resources had to be a priority for the duration of the 4 weeks.

  • Quick access to: Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)
    I expressed the need to have easy and quick access to subject matter experts (SME). These were senior staff members who had been using the legacy system for years and had a deep understanding of its functionalities and limitations.
  • Alignment with Product Owner (PO) and core team
    Given the deadline, I requested daily meetings with the PO and regular meetings with the team in order to understand their UI vision for the product; including time to read and ask questions from their research documents.
