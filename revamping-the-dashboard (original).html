Revamping the Dashboard

for a client’s parking site management application

The client had built an excellent demo application of their SaaS solution but felt it was still visually lacking

“When presenting screenshots of the application to potential buyers, the UI doesn’t easily show all the features it's capable of” --Client

Without usability testing, the solution for “visually lacking” can be dangerously subjective. Lack of usability testing is one of the 4 design constraints that I gathered while scoping the project.

Constraints can tell us how to approach the design process. I decided to first analyse the current application design to understand what content was displayed and how, compare my findings to the product's business logic, and allows the gaps in the comparison to guide the new user interface design.

4 constraints and 4 decisions towards a solution Little qualitative and quantitative usability data to rely on
  • This constraint lead to the decision to analyse the current application as if I were its product owner
No time to arrange user interviews
  • This constraint lead to the decision to interview the stakeholders acting as either a team member or as a new user to the application
Must be in scope of design system
  • This constraint lead to the decision to save time by getting approval of my layouts in low-to-mid fidelity before designing the final design components from scratch
Design team of one, limited to ~4 weeks
  • This constraint is an opportunity to build a relationship with the team behind the product and to understand and adapt to the team dynamics in order to work efficiently

Prioritizing the relevant content

The fewer the actions required by the user and the higher the priority of the task is for the user, the more useful it will be to display the content on the Dashboard.

Monolithic architecture

Borrowing language from programming, the remaining components from the matrix graph above can broadly be seen as monolithic, whereby one menu item (i.e. Space management) relies on the previous one (Site settings) in order to function.

New Dashboard should
  • Guide user around one area of control since all the other areas are too complex (i.e. monolithic architecture)
  • Find opportunity to use spatial assets as a visual component to the application
